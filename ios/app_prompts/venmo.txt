This file describes what typically appears when using Venmo on iOS.

Sending money:
When you open Venmo, you usually see a social-style feed showing recent payments. A prominent action control to Pay/Request appears, often a blue floating button. After selecting it, a search bar appears to find people by name, username, phone, or email. After selecting a recipient, a screen with a numeric keypad for entering amount and a note input appears. A Pay or Request selector is displayed. After entering the amount and note, selecting Pay shows a confirmation with privacy selector and funding source. A final button confirms the payment.

Requesting money:
The flow is similar to sending money. After selecting a recipient and entering an amount and note, selecting Request instead of Pay sends a payment request to the recipient.

Viewing transaction history:
After selecting your profile or the Me tab, a list of your past transactions appears with dates, amounts, recipients, and notes.

Scanning a QR code:
A scan icon may appear on the home screen or pay screen. Selecting it opens the camera to scan another user's Venmo QR code for quick payment.

Searching for a person:
On the pay screen, the search bar accepts names, usernames, phone numbers, or email addresses. Matching contacts appear as you type.

Common situations that may appear:
- Wrong recipient selected with similar names
- Insufficient funds or funding source issue
- Identity verification prompts
- Privacy default not matching user preference
- Payment marked as pending rather than completed

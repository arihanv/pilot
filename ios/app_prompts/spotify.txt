This file describes what typically appears when using Spotify on iOS.

Playing a song or playlist:
When you open Spotify, you usually see a home screen with recently played content, personalized playlists, and recommendations. A bottom navigation bar typically includes Home, Search, and Your Library. After selecting a playlist or album, a list of tracks appears. Selecting a track starts playback. A mini player bar appears at the bottom showing the current track with play/pause controls.

Searching for music:
After selecting the Search tab, a search bar appears at the top with browsing categories below. When you type a query, results appear including songs, artists, albums, playlists, and podcasts.

Creating a playlist:
After selecting Your Library, a list of playlists appears. A Create Playlist button or plus icon allows creating a new playlist. After naming it, you can search for and add songs.

Adding to queue:
While a track is playing, pressing and holding a song or selecting the more options icon shows an Add to Queue option.

Connecting to a speaker or AirPods:
In the Now Playing screen, a devices icon appears. Selecting it shows available devices such as AirPods, Bluetooth speakers, or other Spotify Connect devices. Selecting a device transfers playback.

Liking a song:
In the Now Playing screen or on a track listing, a heart icon appears. Selecting it adds the song to your Liked Songs playlist.

Common situations that may appear:
- Multiple results with similar names
- Shuffle mode activating instead of specific track
- Premium-only content restrictions
- Playback not starting due to connectivity
- Podcast vs music content in search results

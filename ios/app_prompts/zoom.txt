This file describes what typically appears when using Zoom on iOS.

Joining a meeting:
When you open Zoom, you usually see a home screen with options such as New Meeting, Join, Schedule, and Share Screen. After selecting Join, a screen with a field to enter a Meeting ID or personal link name appears. A field to enter your display name may also appear. Options for enabling audio or turning off video before joining may be displayed. A button to join the meeting appears at the bottom. After joining, the meeting interface appears with participant video tiles or speaker view.

Starting a new meeting:
After selecting New Meeting, options such as enabling video appear. After starting, the meeting interface appears with controls for mute, video, participants, chat, share content, and leave meeting.

Scheduling a meeting:
After selecting Schedule, a form appears with fields for meeting name, date, time, duration, and options such as passcode and waiting room. A Save or Done button creates the scheduled meeting.

Sharing screen during a meeting:
During an active meeting, a Share Content button appears in the controls. Selecting it shows options to share your screen, photos, files, or a whiteboard. A broadcast prompt may appear for screen sharing.

Using chat during a meeting:
During an active meeting, a Chat button appears in the controls. Selecting it opens a chat panel where you can type messages to all participants or specific individuals.

Common situations that may appear:
- Waiting room prompts before entering
- Audio connection selection
- Camera and microphone permission prompts
- Host approval requirements
- Meeting password entry
- Recording consent notifications

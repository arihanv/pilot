This file describes what typically appears when using Settings on iOS.

Navigating settings:
When you open Settings, you usually see a scrollable list of categories. Your Apple ID profile appears at the top, followed by sections such as Airplane Mode, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Cellular, Notifications, Sounds, Focus, Screen Time, and General.

Connecting to Wi-Fi:
After selecting Wi-Fi, a list of available networks appears. The currently connected network is shown at the top with a checkmark. You can select a different network to connect. A password entry field appears for secured networks.

Managing Bluetooth devices:
After selecting Bluetooth, a list of paired and available devices appears. You can toggle Bluetooth on or off and connect to a device by selecting it.

Managing notifications:
After selecting Notifications, a list of apps appears. Selecting an app shows notification preferences such as alerts, sounds, badges, and lock screen display.

Managing storage:
After selecting General and then iPhone Storage, a breakdown of storage usage appears. Apps are listed by size. You can select an app to offload it or delete its data.

Updating software:
After selecting General and then Software Update, the current iOS version is displayed. If an update is available, details and a Download and Install button appear.

Managing app permissions:
After selecting Privacy & Security, categories such as Location Services, Camera, Microphone, and Photos appear. Selecting a category shows which apps have access and you can toggle permissions.

Common situations that may appear:
- Wi-Fi password entry prompts
- Bluetooth pairing confirmation
- Software update notifications
- Storage management prompts
- Accessibility options
- Privacy and security settings
